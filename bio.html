My name is Won Lee, and I am a researcher interested in computational statistics and machine learning. In particular, I am focused on statistical textual analysis, NLP, deep learning, and reinforcement learning. Presently, I am a Ph.D. student at Harvard University, and I previously graduated from the same institution having studied physics, mathematics, and computer science.

Here you will find descriptions of current and past projects, links to available publications, and expository and lecture-related material on courses I have taken and taught. In addition, I create and maintain a series of videos dedicated to a rigorous but intuitive understanding of probability, statistics, and machine learning, dubbed Statistics Done Right (StatsDoneRight, a la Sheldon Axler), which you can find at the statsDR link above. Finally, one of my major hobbies is creative writing, and you can find examples of my work in writing.

If you are interested in collaborating on a project or discussing potential ideas, feel free to reach out via links on the bottom of the page. You can also find a brief CV regarding my experiences here.
